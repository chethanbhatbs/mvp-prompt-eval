What this means — the 6 points

  • The fix works. MVP-split gets free users to a preview within 10 credits far more often: Normal 53% → v2 77% / eph3 70%.
  • Biggest win is exactly where it hurt — long prompts. P1: Normal previews at 10–11 ECU (misses the cap on all 3 runs); v2 & eph3 land at ~6 ECU (3/3 within cap).
  • eph3 is the best variant. Highest quality (7.7, wins 3/5 prompts) and previews within cap 70% — the cost win without under-building.
  • v2 is cheapest but risky. Lowest cost, but on the complex Task app its best build scored only 4/10 (thin) — it under-builds to hit the cap.
  • P3 (heavy dashboards) is unsolved. Every arm previews only at 12–22 ECU — over the cap regardless. Needs a separate lever (higher free cap / harder slicing).
  • Reliable & fair. 2,637 LLM calls, 0.3% errors (all timeout-retries, recovered), 0 compaction, spread evenly across arms.
Decision: ship the MVP-split prompt; adopt the eph3 (long-prompt-1) variant. Add a separate plan for P3-class heavy prompts. Re-run live functional E2E once preview pods are healthy (6/6 passed where live).
