Verdict

v2_credit_cap cuts total ECU ~32% across P1–P4 and runs faster, but the saving is uneven and partly from building less. P1 (complex): cheaper but thin (quality 4 vs 6.7). P2 (small): equal quality (8/8) at lower cost — clean win. P3 (large): comparable quality at ~56% less — win. P4: the cap arm spent slightly MORE (avg 20.4 vs 16.2 ECU) and ran extra phases — the MVP-split didn't help on this prompt. Net: v2_credit_cap is a strong default for getting users to a usable result within budget on most prompts, but it under-builds complex apps and shows no benefit on P4.
